Understanding

What is Nisab?

Nisab is the minimum amount of wealth a Muslim must possess before they become liable to pay Zakat.

Which Nisab to Use?

Most scholars recommend using the silver Nisab as it benefits the poor more. However, you may use the gold Nisab for a more conservative calculation.

Using silver Nisab means more people qualify to pay Zakat, increasing charity distribution.

Eligibility

Who Can Receive Zakat?

The Quran specifies eight categories of people eligible to receive Zakat (Surah At-Tawbah 9:60).

1

Fuqara (The Poor)

Those who do not possess enough wealth to meet their basic needs. They have some means but not sufficient for their daily requirements.

2

Masakin (The Needy)

Those who are extremely poor and have no means whatsoever. They are worse off than the Fuqara and depend entirely on charity.

3

Amileen (Zakat Collectors)

Those appointed to collect and distribute Zakat. They receive a reasonable wage for their work from the Zakat funds.

4

Mu'allafatu Qulub

Those whose hearts are to be reconciled. This includes new Muslims or those inclined towards Islam who need support to strengthen their faith.

5

Riqaab (Captives/Slaves)

For freeing slaves or captives. In modern times, this can include paying ransom for Muslims held captive or helping free people from bondage.

6

Gharimeen (Debtors)

Those in debt who cannot pay it off. They must be in genuine distress and the debt should be for permissible needs, not for sinful purposes.

7

Fi Sabeelillah

In the cause of Allah. This includes those striving in the path of Allah, such as students of Islamic knowledge, fighters for just causes, or community welfare projects.

8

Ibnus Sabil (Wayfarers)

Travelers stranded without resources. Even a wealthy person who loses their means while traveling can receive Zakat to return home.

Important Guidelines:

  • Recipients must be Muslim (except some scholars allow non-Muslims under certain conditions)
  • Cannot give Zakat to parents, grandparents, children, or grandchildren
  • Cannot give Zakat to spouse
  • Cannot give Zakat to Sayyid family (descendants of Prophet Muhammad PBUH)
  • Should not give more than 1/3 of Zakat to one recipient
Exemptions

What is Exempt from Zakat?

Understanding what assets are not subject to Zakat is equally important.

Personal Residence

The house you live in is exempt from Zakat. However, if you own multiple properties for investment, Zakat is due on those.

Personal Vehicles

Cars, motorcycles, or any vehicles used for personal transportation are not subject to Zakat.

Household Items

Furniture, appliances, clothing, and other household items for personal use are exempt from Zakat.

Tools of Trade

Tools, machinery, and equipment used for your profession or business are not subject to Zakat on their value.

Books & Knowledge

Books, educational materials, and resources for seeking knowledge are exempt from Zakat.

Jewelry for Use

Some scholars exempt jewelry worn regularly by women, while others include all gold/silver. The safer opinion is to pay on all jewelry.

Haram Income

Income from prohibited sources (interest, alcohol, gambling, etc.) must be disposed of entirely, not as Zakat but as removing haram wealth.

Minor's Wealth

Some schools exempt wealth of minors from Zakat, while others (like Shafi'i) require it. The majority opinion is that Zakat is due.

Note: While these items are exempt from Zakat, they may still be subject to other forms of charity like Sadaqah or Waqf. Always consult with a qualified Islamic scholar for specific situations.

Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

When should I pay Zakat?

Zakat becomes obligatory when you have possessed wealth equal to or above the Nisab for one lunar year (Hawl). You can pay it any time during the year, but it's recommended to pay it as soon as it's due. Many Muslims choose to pay during Ramadan for multiplied rewards.

Can I give Zakat to my relatives?

You can give Zakat to relatives EXCEPT those you are financially responsible for (parents, grandparents, children, grandchildren) and your spouse. Giving to needy relatives like siblings, aunts, uncles, or cousins is actually doubly rewarding as it combines charity with maintaining family ties (Silat ar-Rahim).

What if my wealth fluctuates during the year?

The traditional method is to calculate based on what you possess at the end of your Zakat year, regardless of fluctuations. Some scholars allow you to deduct temporary debts that exist at the time of calculation. If your wealth falls below Nisab at the end of the year, you may not need to pay Zakat.

Is Zakat due on my retirement account (401k, pension)?

Scholars have different opinions. The majority view is that you should pay Zakat on the current value of your retirement accounts, either annually or defer until withdrawal. Some scholars deduct early withdrawal penalties from the calculation. Consult with a knowledgeable scholar for your specific situation.

How do I calculate Zakat on stocks and shares?

For stocks held for investment, pay 2.5% on the market value. If you're a trader (buying/selling frequently), it's treated as business inventory. Some scholars differentiate between the company's liquid assets vs. fixed assets to determine a reduced rate, but the simplest method is 2.5% on total market value.

Can I pay Zakat in advance or in installments?

Yes, you can pay Zakat in advance before your Zakat date. This is permissible and even recommended for those who can. You can also pay in installments throughout the year, as long as the total is paid by your due date. Many Muslims set up monthly payments to charity organizations for this purpose.
